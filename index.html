Key Findings

Five things costing you customers right now.

⏱

Customers leave before the menu even loads

On mobile, your homepage takes several seconds before anything meaningful appears — photos, menus, hours. Most visitors decide within 2–3 seconds. You're losing people the moment they arrive, before they've seen a single thing about your restaurant.

High Impact
🔍

Google can't find your Private Events page

When someone searches "private party venue Houston Heights" or "book event space near Memorial Park," your booking pages don't appear in results. People with event budgets — corporate parties, birthday dinners, rehearsal lunches — are finding your competitors instead of you, every single month.

High Impact
📱

You're ignoring roughly 30% of your calls

Across six venues, a significant share of inbound contact comes as texts or expects a text-back. Right now that channel is essentially silent. Catering inquiries, reservation confirmations, event questions — whoever responds fastest wins the booking. That's often not you.

High Impact
📖

Your best marketing asset is buried at the bottom

The Guadalupe River origin story — cold beers on a tube, Texas heat, friends deciding to bring that feeling to Houston — is genuinely compelling. It currently lives near the bottom of your homepage where virtually no one scrolls. That story is why someone chooses you over a generic bar. It should be the first thing they feel.

Medium Impact
🔗

Six brands with no connective tissue online

Each location has its own site, which is fine. But there's no digital hub that communicates the full picture to a first-time visitor: that this is a 20-year Houston institution with six distinct personalities. Cross-location loyalty, gift cards that work everywhere, event packages across venues — none of this is visible or easy to access.

Bottom Line

The foundation is already there.

The Creek Group has 20+ years of genuine Houston credibility, a compelling origin story, and six venues that people actually love. The highest-return moves are not about reinvention — they're about making sure the digital front door reflects what's already behind it. Faster load, better search visibility, and closing the SMS gap would be the three moves that pay back fastest.
